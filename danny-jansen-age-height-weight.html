Danny Jansen Age, Height, Weight: Danny Jansen, officially known as Daniel Robert Jansen was born on April 15, 1995.

At the time of this writing (Thursday, May 18, 2023), he’s an American professional baseball catcher for the Toronto Blue Jays of Major League Baseball (MLB).

Jansen developed a passion for playing baseball at a younger age and has been consistent throughout his career rising through the ranks to become one of the most sought-after baseball players.

He was drafted by the Blue Jays in the 16th round of the 2013 Major League Baseball draft while attending Appleton West High School in Appleton, Wisconsin

Initially, Jansen had committed to play college baseball at Jacksonville University but instead signed with the Blue Jays.

He was then assigned to the Rookie-level Gulf Coast League Blue Jays for the season, appearing in 36 games and hitting .246 with 18 runs batted in (RBI).

In 2014, Jansen was promoted to the Rookie Advanced Bluefield Blue Jays, and in 38 games, he batted .282 with five home runs and 17 RBI.

On January 12, 2016, he was invited to the Major League spring training and reassigned to minor league camp on March 12 of that same year (2016).

Prior to the start of the 2017 season, Jansen found he was having vision problems, and began wearing glasses on and off the field.

The Blue Jays promoted Jansen to the major leagues on August 12, 2018, and subsequently made his debut in a 3–1 loss to the Kansas City Royals

The 2021 season saw Jansen playing 70 games. He spent time on the injured list in July and August with a right hamstring strain.

He returned to the lineup to play the last 21 games of the season with a .322 batting average and 7 home runs.

In July 2022, the Blue Jays set a franchise record for runs scored in a game with a 28-5 win over the Boston Red Sox at Fenway Park.

Jansen hit 2 home runs over the Green Monster in left field, scored 4 times, and contributed 6 RBIs.

On January 13, 2023, Jansen signed a one-year, $3.5 million contract with the Blue Jays, avoiding salary arbitration.
