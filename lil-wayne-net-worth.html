Early Life

Lil Wayne was born Dwayne Michael Carter Jr. on September the 27th, 1982, in the Hollygrove neighborhood of New Orleans, Louisiana, United States.

His mother, Jacida Carter, gave birth to him when she was just 19 years of age. His father permanently abandoned the family after a divorce.

After that, his mother married late Reginald “Rabbit” McDonald. Wayne has stated that he considers his deceased stepfather as his real father. In fact, he has a tattoo dedicated to his step-father.

As for his education, Wayne first enrolled in the gifted program at Lafayette Elementary School and later switched to Eleanor McMain Secondary School.

He stayed in school for two years, where he was a member of the drama club and also was an honor student.

Later, he enrolled in Marion Abramson Senior High School only to drop out at the age of 14 to focus entirely on his musical career.

Wayne wrote his first rap song when he was eight years of age. He met his mentor Bryan “Birdman” Williams in the summer of 1991, who encouraged his love for hip-hop.

Wayne almost died because of a self-inflicted gunshot wound in 1994 at the age of 12.
